Our Platform: Services That Actually Work

The Waterloo Engineering Society exists to "promote a positive undergraduate experience." We're here to make that promise a reality.

1 Modernize the Exam Bank & Academic Resources

No more searching through decade-old exams. We will build a modern, searchable exam bank with recent materials for every core course.

How we'll do it:
  • Launch recurring "Exam Bank Submission Drives" with meaningful incentives (C&D gift cards, POETS vouchers, EngSoc merch)
  • Work with professors to release modern midterms and finals with appropriate permissions
  • Develop a cleaner, searchable interface ensuring every popular course has recent exam materials
Why it matters: Better preparation means better performance.

2 Enhance Career Fair & On-Site Interviews

Transform the Career Fair from just networking to real recruitment with on-site interview opportunities.

How we'll do it:
  • Secure on-site or near-site interviews at our Career Fair
  • Introduce micro-networking sessions targeted by discipline (software, hardware, energy, consulting)
  • Increase professional-development workshops (interview prep, company-specific training)
Why it matters: Direct pathways to internships and full-time positions.

3 Strengthen Departmental Societies & Design Teams

Build stronger connections between all engineering groups through formal support and resource sharing.

How we'll do it:
  • Continue building the Interdepartmental Societies Council
  • Provide shared resources and streamlined communication channels
  • Ensure every department has representation in EngSoc's service ecosystem
Why it matters: Stronger communities mean better support for every student.

4 Embed Sustainability in All Services

Sustainability isn't an add-on â€” it's embedded in everything we do, from event planning to procurement.

How we'll do it:
  • Implement sustainability audits for all events and services
  • Reduce single-use plastics at career fairs and events
  • Support student groups working on climate and energy initiatives
  • Create a "Sustainability Checklist" for all EngSoc directors
Why it matters: Engineering the future means protecting it too.

5 Explore Campus Nap Pods & Wellness Spaces

Recognizing the intensive demands on engineering students, we'll research and implement wellness spaces on campus.

How we'll do it:
  • Research feasibility of nap pods/quiet wellness rooms near POETS and E7
  • Evaluate funding options (ECIF, WEEF, sponsorships)
  • Run student surveys to gauge interest and optimal locations
Why it matters: Supporting student well-being improves academic performance and mental health.
